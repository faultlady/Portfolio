Predicting the concentration of Chlorophyll-a in Arabian Sea using Satellite images on R

The aim of this study is to study the patterns of distribution and concentration of chl-a over a time period from 1993 to 2019 in Indian waters using R. Recognition of rocks under thin section using Deep Learning

They can be distinguished on a macro and micro scale, owing to their unique surface structures. However, investigation on a micro scale using specially designed petrographic microscopes is a reliable way to pass on a verdict on the details of the rock. Unless one has very sophisticated instruments like a petrographic microscope or advanced instruments like electron microscope, that only rich organisations can afford, a detailed and more reliable study of the rock cannot be made.

An effort has been made to train a Deep Learning model to, as of now, to simply recognize the kind of rock it is (igneous/sedimentary/metamorphic) using the images of rocks in thin section, that is how rock would look when sliced down into a thin section enough to put on a microscope stage and further observed by a Geoscientist.
